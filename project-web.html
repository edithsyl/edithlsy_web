Some might say having a portfolio website is a must for aspiring programmers and desginers... However, I found the portfolio website templates avaliable online a bit boring and don't fit my personality well. Therefore, I decided to design and develop one for my own.

Problem Statement

Nowadays, almost everyone has a portfolio website. I need one to showcase my skills and past projects to increase my chance of getting a nice job.

Planning

Any UXUI couse would teach you the first step is to research and plan. And according to Google Foundation of User Experience:

In the field of user experience design, the ‚Äúuser‚Äù comes first.

Well said Google, well said. (please hire me someday?)
So I try to come up with two personas that are gonna visit my website

Persona 1: The Recruiter

Possibly a middle age guy, who is smart, handsome and likes to get things done efficiently.

Persona 2: The Client

Someone youthful and green, looking for peers to collaborate with. She is open-minded, self-driven and have a vision or project in mind.

Need: Figure out my abilities and determine if I would be good at my job with the shortest time possible

Summary:
- make my website easier to comprehend, so people could find key information quickly
- to better showcase my skills, I should put more effort in explaining my past projects and what I have learned through the process

What about me?

I want my website to be in line with my personalities. So I did a little soul-searching research about myself:

I would describe myself as creative, with quirky humour, passionate

I hope my website could be homely and warm, like a scribbled notebook. But it should be clean and easy to read all the same.
